Atmosphere

A world that feels alive, not just colorful.

The visuals in Harvest It lean into the pleasure of movement — fields swaying, produce popping with color, and a clear sense of progress as you clear space. The sound design matters too: that crisp slice of the cutter, the soft ambience, the gentle music underneath. It’s immersive without being overwhelming, which is why it works for both short sessions and long, satisfying streaks. Sometimes I'm trying to get perfect runs, other times I just zone out with it.
